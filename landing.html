Why Families Choose Sampada

Clear positioning for a tracking-first product

What Sampada is

  • Consolidated income, expense, and investment tracking
  • Family-level visibility for net worth and cash flow
  • Simple records of assets like MF, stocks, FD, and PPF

What Sampada is not

  • Not a brokerage account
  • Not a trading terminal
  • Not a financial product marketplace

Data Scope & Transparency

Clear expectations on data sources and update cadence

Automated market data

  • Mutual funds via public MF APIs and scheme datasets
  • Daily NAV refresh based on previous market day close
  • Stocks sourced from NSE public endpoints

Manual tracking modules

  • Income entries are manually maintained
  • Expense tracking is manually maintained
  • Fixed deposits and similar instruments are manually maintained

Account Aggregator (AA) integration is currently not available. Data is sourced from selected public market endpoints and user-maintained entries.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers users expect before signing up

Does Sampada support trading or order placement?

No. Sampada is a tracking and reporting platform only.

Who is this built for?

Individuals and families who want one place to monitor money, assets, and goals.

How long does setup take?

Most households can set up their first dashboard in about 5 minutes.

Do you connect via Account Aggregator (AA)?

Not currently. The platform combines selected public market data with manual entries.

Which values are automated vs manually entered?

Mutual fund and stock references are refreshed from public sources, while income, expenses, and fixed deposits are manually maintained.

Will pricing remain free forever?

Early access is free. If paid plans are introduced, pricing will be based on feature access and storage limits, not a reduced quality of core service.

How quickly can I expect support responses?

Support is currently founder-led. Replies are handled personally and may be delayed during high request volume.
